# Target Users

## Primary User Groups

### 1. County Emergency Management Officials

- **Role**: Coordinate flood response and mitigation efforts at the county level.
- **Needs**:
  - Real-time flood monitoring dashboards.
  - Integration with existing emergency management systems.
  - Data-driven decision support tools.
  - Communication tools for public alerts.

### 2. Local Residents and Homeowners

- **Role**: Individuals living in flood-prone areas.
- **Needs**:
  - Personalized flood risk assessments.
  - Timely alerts via multiple channels (SMS, email, app notifications).
  - Easy-to-understand flood maps and visualizations.
  - Community reporting tools for local observations.

### 3. Community Leaders and Volunteers

- **Role**: Organize local response efforts and support vulnerable populations.
- **Needs**:
  - Tools for coordinating volunteer efforts.
  - Access to community-sourced data.
  - Training resources for flood preparedness.
  - Communication platforms for neighborhood coordination.

### 4. First Responders (Fire, Police, EMS)

- **Role**: Provide immediate response to flood emergencies.
- **Needs**:
  - Real-time situational awareness.
  - Integration with dispatch systems.
  - Access to flood-specific protocols and resources.
  - Communication with other agencies.

## Secondary User Groups

### 5. Environmental Agencies

- **Role**: Monitor environmental impacts and long-term flood trends.
- **Needs**:
  - Historical flood data analysis.
  - Integration with environmental monitoring systems.
  - Research and planning tools.

### 6. Insurance Companies

- **Role**: Assess flood risk for underwriting and claims.
- **Needs**:
  - Accurate flood risk data.
  - Historical loss data.
  - Predictive modeling tools.

### 7. Urban Planners and Engineers

- **Role**: Design flood mitigation infrastructure.
- **Needs**:
  - Detailed flood modeling data.
  - Scenario planning tools.
  - Integration with GIS systems.

## User Characteristics

### Technical Proficiency

- **Low**: Many residents may have limited technical skills.
- **Medium**: Emergency officials and first responders have moderate technical training.
- **High**: Environmental agencies and urban planners are highly technical.

### Access Patterns

- **Mobile-First**: Residents and first responders need mobile access.
- **Desktop/Web**: Officials and planners prefer web-based interfaces.
- **24/7 Access**: Critical for emergency situations.

### Data Needs

- **Real-time**: Immediate alerts and current conditions.
- **Historical**: Trend analysis and planning.
- **Predictive**: Forecasting and risk assessment.

This diverse user base requires a flexible, scalable system that can adapt to different technical capabilities and use cases while maintaining ease of use for all stakeholders.
